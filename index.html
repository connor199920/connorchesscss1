Connor's Chess Website

This website links to the best chess resources and explains why you should use them. By the end of this guide, you'll be able to navigate all the major chess websites online and understand which is best for you. I started playing chess around 3 years ago, and since then I have been continually looking for new chess websites and resources to help improve my game. The 4 websites below I have found to be the most helpful in my development. All of these websites have free aspects, but some do come with premium features you need to pay for. The websites below are ranked from best to worst in my opinion, but I do think all of these websites are great, and you should give each one a try.

Explore the Chess Platforms:

Website Brief Descriptions

  1. Chess.com is the biggest chess website of all with 2 million daily players, but it costs money for extra features.
  2. Lichess is Chess.com's main competitor. Lichess is a completely free, community-funded site with 150,000 daily players.
  3. Chessable is a website that helps you work on your openings. Players like Magnus Carlsen and Hikaru Nakamura highly recommend this site.
  4. Chessly is a website created by GothamChess and offers lots of courses, including openings, middlegame strategies, and endgame tactics.
